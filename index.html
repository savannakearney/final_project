Food

Faneuil Hall

Located near the waterfront of Boston, Faneuil Hall has been a marketplace since 1743. Besides being home to 14 different restaurants, bars and pubs, Quincy Market Colonnade also resides here, featuring more than 36 international vendors in the largest food hall in New England. Price: $$.

Mike's Pastry

Founded in 1946, Mike's Pastry is one of the best-known Italian bakeries in Boston. Tourists and locals alike will recognize the iconic white box with a bright blue label and bow tied with a string, containing anything from cannolis, lobster tails, black and white cookies or Boston cream pie. >Price: $.

History

Image courtesy of Marco Krenn on Unsplash.

Freedom Trail

Walk through the very beginning of United States history on the Freedom Trail. This 2.5-mile route leads visitors to 16 different historical sites throughout Boston. Tour guides are dressed in authentic colonial garb as they lead you through the American Revolution. Price: $12 for adults/$10 for students.

Salem

Located less than an hour north of Boston, the city of Salem was where the infamous Salem Witch Trials took place. Although those events took place in the 1690s, Salem still offers a look into the past with museums, witch houses, gardens and more. Price: varies from $10-$15 per house.

Plymouth Rock

Nothing is more historical than the spot where the first pilgrims from the Mayflower landed in the New World in 1620. A portion of Plymouth Rock now rests on display at Plymouth Harbor, next to a replica of the original Mayflower ship, the Mayflower II. Both of these pieces of history are free to view, and located just over an hour south of Boston. Price: free.

Fun

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Boston Common

Dating from 1634, Boston Common is the oldest city park in the U.S. Besides being a park rich in history, there are often free concerts and other events at the commons, depending on the season. Price: free.

Fenway Park

Home of the Boston Redsox since 1953, Fenway Park is the oldest ballpark in Major League Baseball. Besides going to Redsox games, you can also visit the Fenway Park Living Museum. Price of park tours: $20. Price of game tickets: varies.

Food

Hammerheads

This gastropub located in the "Germantown" district of Louisville can be easy to miss, but it boasts a diverse craft beer, barbeque and burgers. And, their Mac 'n' Cheese Balls are to die for. Price: $$.

Safai Coffeeshop

This unique coffeeshop, opened by a couple in 1998 in the Highlands area of Louisville may seem small, but that's the local charm. The menu boasts a range of organic coffees, drinks, and even delicious crepes. Price: $.

Outdoors

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Red River Gorge

Although it's a bit of a drive, this canyon system in the Daniel Boone National Forest is completely worth it. There's a plethora of things to explore here, including cliffs, hiking, waterfalls, caves and a stunning Natural Bridge, a sandstone rock bridge that spans 78 feet and is 65 feet high. It's particularly worth the long hike if you make it in time for sunset. Price: free.

Mammoth Cave

This cave is called mammoth for a reason. Located about an hour outside of the city, it is the world's longest known cave system. Price: $7.

The Big Four Bridge

Who says you can't spend time outdoors in the city? This bridge is a former railroad truss bridge that spans the Ohio River, connecting Louisville, Kentucky with Jeffersonville, Indiana. It was constructed in 1895, but it was converted into a pedestrian and bicycle bridge in 1969. Price: free.

Fun

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Churchill Downs

Although it's the thoroughbred racetrack known for hosting the Kentucky Derby, Chirchill Downs is open to the public for watching races during the rest of the year as well. And tickets are just $3! Although it's up to you how much you spend betting on horses. Price: $3.

Belle of Louisville

The Belle of Louisville is the oldest Mississippi River steamboat still in operation, and it is a U.S. National Historic Landmark. Launched in 1914, it was used as a ferry between Memphis, TN and West Memphis, AR. And, April through October you can board for river cruises ato view the beautiful sites of the city. Price: $15-$43.

Charleston, South Carolina

Image courtesy of Spencer Means on Flickr.

Food

Image courtesy of Eaters Collective on Unsplash.

Poogan's Porch

This quaint restaurant is all about southern tradition. Poogan's Porch transforms from Sunday brunch on the porch to elegant drinks and rich handmade cuisine at night. Price: $$.

Stars Rooftop and Grill Room

Experience the nightlife of Charleston at Stars, a rooftop bar and American grill, located on King Street. The rooftop bar offers a 360 degree view of the historic district of Charleston, and downstairs boasts a 1930s style grill room that creates unique and delectable food. Price: $$.

Fun

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Magnolia Plantation

Founded in 1676 by the Drayton family, this is Charleston's most visited plantation. The plantation opened its doors to the public in 1870 and since then offers stunning gardens, a zoo, maze cafe and tours of the old plantation house. If you're looking to save money, November through January you can buy one admission and get one free by bringing a non-perishable food donation. Admission price: $20 plus extra tour costs.

Charleston City Market

Charleston City Market was established in the 1790s and stretches four city blocks through downtown Charleston. As one of the nation's oldest public market, it is home to more than 300 entrepreneurs and vendors, so you'll always be able to take home a souvenir or two. Price: free.

Architecture

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Rainbow Row

This is the name for 13 colorful historic houses located along East Bay Street. Although they're not pen to the public, they make for some pretty great photo-ops. Price: free.

The Battery

The Battery is the name for East Battery Street, home to some of the grandest antebellum mansions in the city. This scenic street sits right across from the water where Cooper River and Ashley River meet, and offers a glimpse into some important Charleston history. Price: free.

Savannah, Georgia

Image courtesy of Jessiva Furtney on Unsplash.

Food

Image courtesy of Yvonne Lee Harijanto on Unsplash.

Treylor Park

Quality over quantity is the motto of this unique Savannah restaurant. Their food is as fun and quirky as it is delicious, and they offer a diverse cocktail and craft beer menu. Price: $$.

The Coffee Fox

The Coffee Fox is a craft coffeeshop, featuring locally-brewed coffee, pastries, cheese boards, wine and beer, as well as a fun, retro-style atmosphere. Price: $.

River Street Sweets

River Street Sweets is the best place to find handmade southern candies. It's a local place that's been run by four generations of family since 1973. Their best seller is the pecan pralines (and they offer free samples at the door!). Price: $$.

Shopping

Image courtesy of Sonja Langford.

Savannah Bee Company

With two locations in downtwon Savannah alone, this adorable honey shop is hard to miss. They sell all things honey related, from raw honecomb, to honey-infused wine coffee, to honey candles, lotions, beauty products and more. Price: $$.

River Street

If shopping isn't enough for you, why not do it on the waterfront? River Street offersa plethora of shops and restaurants, all along cobblestone streets right across from Savannah River. There's even sometimes street vendors and performers along River street, so you'll never get bored. Price: $-$$$.

History

Image courtesy of Savanna Kearney.

Cathedral of St. John the Baptist

Haitian and French immigrants established this first parish in Savannah in the late 1700s. The Cathedral of St. John the Baptist is a stunning Catholic cathedral and an icon of Savannah. You can take a self-guided tour for a $2 donation during weekdays or even attend daily Sunday Masses. Price: free-$2.

Forsyth Park

Forsyth Park is the oldest park in the historic district of Savannah. It's more than 160 years old and spans 30 acres. This park features concerts, a Saturday farmers market, beautiful foliage, a historic monument and an impressive fountain at the north end of the park. Price: free.
